The truth about limits
for tuba and live electronics
2013

There is a unique challenge that faces players of single line instruments. Works like the solo suites for Bach require the player to convey sustained chords without being able to literally play them. The instruments that these pieces were written for likely had longer sustain than modern bowed string instruments. Viola da gamba players make use of this fact by holding down notes in their left hands long after the bow has left the string achieving a chordal effect.

Wind players do not really have the same kind of tricks at their disposal. This piece gives a tuba player access to sustained notes in the way that a pianist can by using the pedal.
