The forest for the trees
for string quartet
2012

Rather than using musical textures, this piece explores superimposition of a single simple motive: a long note followed by a short one. In place of motivic development or melodic construction, the interest in the piece comes from exploration of range and density. Thus, the listener is meant to appreciate the general trajectory of the counterpoint instead of the surface level details of the music. In other words to enjoy this piece, the listener must not miss the forest for the trees
